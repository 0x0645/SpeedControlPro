## Design Context

### Users
General video consumers and students/learners who watch content on YouTube, Netflix, educational platforms, and other video sites. They want to optimize viewing time without fiddling with settings on every visit. Context: mid-task (watching a lecture, bingeing a series) — the extension must stay out of the way until needed, then be immediately clear.

### Brand Personality
**Polished, quiet, confident.** Feels like a premium product even though it's free. Not flashy — refined. The kind of tool you trust immediately because it looks like someone cared about every detail.

Three words: **Refined. Effortless. Precise.**

Emotional goal: The user should feel *in control* without feeling like they're operating a complex tool.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Minimal & neutral.** Strip back to essentials — let function lead. Muted, restrained palette. Monochrome base with a single purposeful accent color. Clean typography with clear hierarchy. Generous whitespace. No decoration that doesn't communicate.

**Anti-references (must NOT look like):**
- Generic Chrome extensions with default Bootstrap/Material styling
- Over-designed apps with excessive animations, gradients, glassmorphism — anything that feels like showing off rather than helping
- AI-generated UI (cyan-on-dark, purple gradients, glowing accents, card-heavy layouts)

**References (spirit, not copy):**
- Arc browser's settings — clean, confident, opinionated
- Linear's UI density — lots of info, never cluttered
- Apple's system preferences — clear hierarchy, invisible design system

### Design Principles

1. **Invisible until needed.** The overlay, popup, and options page should feel like natural extensions of the browser — not separate apps demanding attention.
2. **Clarity over cleverness.** Every element earns its place. No decorative gradients, no animations that don't serve comprehension, no redundant labels.
3. **One glance, full understanding.** Visual hierarchy must be strong enough that a user can scan the popup in under 2 seconds and know the current speed and how to change it.
4. **Density without clutter.** Per-site profiles and shortcuts are complex features — present them with enough breathing room to feel approachable, but compact enough to not overwhelm.
5. **Consistent restraint.** Same spacing, same radius, same type scale everywhere. The design system should be so consistent it becomes invisible.
